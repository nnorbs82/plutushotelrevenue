Hotel Photography Tips

Capture your property in the best light to drive bookings

High-quality photography is one of the most important factors in a guest's booking decision. Follow these professional tips to showcase your hotel effectively.

1. Prepare Your Space

Before the photoshoot, ensure everything is perfect:

  • Deep clean all areas to be photographed
  • Remove personal items and clutter
  • Check that all light fixtures are working
  • Ensure bed linens are crisp and perfectly made
  • Stage rooms with subtle decorative elements
  • Hide electrical cords and modern technology when possible

2. Master Natural Lighting

Lighting can make or break your hotel photos:

  • Shoot during the "golden hour" (early morning or late afternoon)
  • Open curtains and blinds to let in natural light
  • Turn on all interior lights to create warmth
  • Balance natural and artificial lighting for best results
  • Avoid harsh midday sun that creates strong shadows
  • Use reflectors to bounce light into darker areas

3. Choose the Right Angles

Composition is key to showcasing your property:

  • Shoot from corners to capture more of the room
  • Keep the camera at chest height for natural perspective
  • Use leading lines to draw the eye through the space
  • Capture both wide shots and detail shots
  • Include shots from multiple angles of each space
  • Show the view from windows if it's appealing

4. Highlight Key Features

Make sure to capture what makes your hotel special:

  • Photograph unique architectural elements
  • Showcase premium amenities (pool, spa, gym, restaurant)
  • Capture the ambiance of common areas
  • Highlight luxury touches and details
  • Show variety in room types and categories
  • Include photos of special event spaces

5. Create a Shot List

Essential photos every hotel should have:

  • Exterior shots from multiple angles
  • Lobby and reception area
  • Each room category (at least 5-7 photos per type)
  • Bathroom close-ups showing fixtures and amenities
  • Restaurant, bar, and dining areas
  • Pool, fitness center, and spa facilities
  • Meeting rooms and event spaces
  • Unique features and architectural details

6. Use Professional Equipment

Invest in quality tools for better results:

  • Use a DSLR or mirrorless camera with a wide-angle lens
  • Invest in a sturdy tripod for sharp, level images
  • Consider a remote shutter release to avoid camera shake
  • Use an external flash for balanced lighting
  • Shoot in RAW format for maximum editing flexibility
  • Have backup batteries and memory cards ready

7. Edit Professionally

Post-processing can enhance your images significantly:

  • Correct white balance for accurate colors
  • Adjust exposure and contrast moderately
  • Straighten lines and correct perspective distortion
  • Remove minor blemishes and imperfections
  • Maintain consistency in color and style across all photos
  • Avoid over-editing that misrepresents the property

8. Consider Hiring a Professional

When to invest in professional photography services:

  • For major property updates or renovations
  • When launching new marketing campaigns
  • If your current photos are outdated or low quality
  • For special occasion or lifestyle photography
  • When you need 360-degree virtual tours
  • If you lack the time or expertise for DIY photography
